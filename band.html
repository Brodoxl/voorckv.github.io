The Band

Meet The Function

Felix Moon

Drummer

The heartbeat of The Function, Felix's thunderous drumming provides the foundation for the band's energetic sound. Known for his wild performances and technical precision, he's been with the band since its formation in 1973.

Theo Lux

Lead Guitar

With his distinctive guitar style, Theo creates the sonic landscapes that define The Function's sound. His innovative techniques and political lyrics have made him a cult figure in the art-rock scene.

Élise Pommet

Lead Vocalist

The voice of The Function, Élise's powerful vocals and charismatic stage presence have made her an icon of the post-punk movement. Her lyrics often address political and social issues with raw honesty.

Fred Vanderbelt-Guinney

Support Vocalist & Bass

Fred's driving basslines and backing vocals add depth to The Function's sound. With a background in classical music, he brings a unique perspective to the band's compositions.

Simon

Multi-instrumentalist

Simply known as Simon, this enigmatic figure handles everything else in The Function. From keyboards to saxophone, his versatility and experimental approach have been crucial to the band's evolving sound.

Our Story

The Function formed in the vibrant musical landscape of early 70s London, when five distinct personalities united with a common vision: to challenge the status quo through art and music. Their first rehearsals took place in a cramped basement in East London, where they forged their unique sound – a blend of punk energy, art-rock experimentation, and politically charged lyrics.

By 1975, their debut album captured the attention of critics and fans alike, establishing The Function as pioneers in the emerging post-punk scene. Through the decades, the band has maintained its rebellious spirit, continuing to push boundaries and challenge norms with each new release.

Despite the changing musical landscape, The Function has stayed true to their roots, refusing to compromise their artistic vision or political stance. Their influence can be heard in countless bands that followed, cementing their legacy as true innovators.

Now, with their latest album "Riot, Riot, Riot," The Function proves they're as relevant and revolutionary as ever, channeling the same anger and energy that defined their early work while exploring new sonic territories.
